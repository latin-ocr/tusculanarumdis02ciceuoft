It is characteristic of Cic. to gibe at the
want of Greek scholarship shown by the
Roman Epicureans (cf. i 3, 6; ii 3, 7;
iv 3, 7, etc.)‚ and there is a grim humour
in selecting the coterie who laid such
stress upon pleasures of the senses, as
examples of persons deprived of pleasure
(and wisdom) ‘at one entrance.’ Epi-
curei nostri and Graeci, moreover‚ give
a better point to omnes nos below.
